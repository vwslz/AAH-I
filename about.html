Kakuma Refugee Camp

Africa is the continent with the largest number of refugees in the world. As one of the most developed countries in East Africa, Kenya also hosts nearly 480,000 refugees from 15 countries. Nearly 80 percent of the refugees are from Somalia and south Sudan, more than 50 percent are minors, and 77 percent are women and children. Kakuma, a town in the northwest corner of Kenya, has had a refugee camp since 1992. Nearly 190,000 refugees live here, about 40 percent of Kenya's total.

Who We Are

AAH-I is an international non-governmental organisation working with communities in the Horn of Africa, and East and Southern Africa to sustainably improve their quality of life. AAH-I has over 30 years' experience working with communities, including refugees, internally displaced people, host communities, pastoralists and people living in urban informal settlements. AAH-I has country programmes in South Sudan, Zambia, Uganda, Somalia, Djibouti, Ethiopia and Kenya.

Mission

As part of restoring the refugee women dignity and allowing them to leave a normal life away from home, Africa action help (AAH-I)in partnership with UNHCR and other partners are organising the women into cooperatives, a providing space for work, skills development by refining and helping them blend the cultural skills and contemporary art. They provide refugees with training in the manufacture and design of handicrafts and clothing, and hopes to use media and social networks to promote refugees' handicrafts and clothing to the global market, so as to help refugees achieve economic independence. For a variety of reasons, refugees are confined to the camps and unable to freely engage in economic activities. Therefore, in addition to mastering handicrafts and garment making skills, AAH-I cooperation with China house to set up this online sales website to help refugees achieve economic independence.
